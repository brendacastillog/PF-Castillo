My Honest Thoughts

A Monster Calls

by Patrick Ness

A perfect portrayal of grief in all of it's forms. It's a very emotional story, it makes you cry for all sorts of different reasons unreleated to the story's main problem.

The Raven Boys

by Maggie Stiefvater

The only regret I have with this book is not reading it sooner. "The Raven Boys" is such a fresh take on magic realism that it just feels normal for the characters to be on the look out for a king's lost tomb. It's a very promising first book for the saga and I'm very happy I gave it a chance.

Shadow and Bone

by Leigh Bardugo

I was conned with this book. The Netflix series did such a good job of selling this story to me and I completely fell for it. The only thing salvable in this book is the intricate world building, Leigh Bardugo made sure everything in her magic system and her fantasy world made sense. Outside of that, I loathed everything.

The Seven Husbands of Evelyn Hugo

by Taylor Jenkins Reid

This is one of the few books that Tiktok recommended me that I actually really liked. It's an interesting story of the ficticious star, Evelyn Hugo, her rise to fame and everything she had to risk in between to get there, family, integrity, and love.
